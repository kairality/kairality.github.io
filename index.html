Bio

Hi there! I'm a software engineer based in San Francisco! I really like fixing bugs and building things that help people connect to, understand, and appreciate the world they live in. I am especially interested in technology that helps people learn about the natural world and other cultures. because when we learn about the natural world we are driven to protect it, and when we learn about other cultures we can recognize our biases and become better people. I am also a fast learner and really enjoy learning new technologies to solve problems!

My story in tech is somewhat untraditional.

Once upon a time, I approached my Number Theory professor after receiving an unexpected programming assignment on the first day of class.

"Excuse me, Dr. Brown, I don't know a programming language. It wasn't listed as a prerequisite for this course."

"Oh." He replied, "Well, it's not due 'til wednesday."

I did some research on programming languages and decided Python was a good place to start. So spent my week learning to code (if my life were an '80s movie, there definitely would have been a montage) and wrote my first computer program: a Python implementation of the sieve of Eratosthenes algorithm for finding prime numbers.

And they all lived happily ever after (or at least, I got an A in the course!)

Eventually I would move on (and across the continent!) and join Facebook in a non-technical role on a brand-new team. However, I noticed there was not always enough engineering time to address all of the requests and suggestions received by the team. It was time to pull another Number Theory.

Using all the resources I could find internally and externally, I taught myself enough PHP and version control to start hacking on our team's tools and build out requested features and improvements.

Over time I would pick up and use a variety of technologies for my role: PHP / Hack, R, SQL, Javascript, React— I even designed and built my own ETL pipelines with my old friend Python and Apache Hive.

Since my role was a hybrid engineering / ops role, I got to do a little bit of everything and served as a bridge between the technical and non-technical sides of my team. But I was always happiest when writing code and building cool things.

After leaving Facebook during the uncertainty of the pandemic, I decided to hone my skills by attending App Academy's software engineering bootcamp to learn more about building applications from scratch. So not only do I come with the grit of a bootcamper, I also come with 6+ years of experience working on dynamic teams in the tech industry!

P.S. Behind me that's a blooming titan arum, the plant with the largest single inflorescence in the world!

Experience

Search Ranking Specialist - Facebook (2014-2020)

This role was a bit of everything - from coding to DevOps to program and people management, I had to do it all!

  • Built and maintained internal tools (PHP / Javascript / React) used by a large contingent workforce and internal product teams
  • Designed and built internal tools for internal teams and vendors to track data quality including visualizers for accuracy, inter-rater reliability, confusion matrices, precision / recall, and disagreements.
  • Participated in a full 24/7 engineering on-call rotation. I won a “fix of the month” award for quickly spinning up a framework to prevent user privacy policy violations in our platform while on the worst in-fight wifi connection somewhere over the Pacific Ocean on the way to Singapore.
  • Created a logging and reporting system, ETL pipeline, and tool to automate time-tracking and billing saving millions of dollars from waste.
  • Managed and developed training programs for a team of 40+ onsite contingent workers, overseeing cross-functional relationships with vendors and other teams.

App Academy (Jan 2022 - July 2022)

I wanted to learn more about the product cycle and building user-facing applications from the ground up, so after some of the pandemic winds quieted a bit I attended App Academy's full-stack engineering bootcamp. I was hesitant at first because I was coming in with some experience, but in the end I was surprised by how much I learned and how much I benefitted from the immersive environment and thousands of hours of pair programming.

You can see some of the things I built during my time there in the Projects section!

Group project with Brendan Downing, Maica Santos, and Attiya Kovenberg. This is a smart to-do list app for dog owners, inspired by Remember the Milk. Notably, the main app is a single-page application built with pure javascript DOM manipulation, no front-end library such as React or Vue was allowed to be used. We were not required to do a SPA, but we went for the challenge anyway.
